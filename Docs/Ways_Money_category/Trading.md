# Trading

Trading focuses on short-term price movements (minutes to weeks) rather than long-term growth. For those starting small or looking for a career, here is how to make money through active trading. [1, 2, 3, 4]

## Small Savings ($100 or Less)

- **Forex Day Trading:** The foreign exchange market is ideal for small accounts due to high liquidity and leverage. With $100, you can trade "micro-lots" (1,000 units) of major pairs like EUR/USD to keep risk low.
- **Fractional Share Trading:** Use apps like [Questrade](https://www.questrade.com/) or Moomoo to trade portions of expensive stocks for as little as $1. This allows you to scalp or swing trade high-value stocks without needing thousands of dollars.
- **Copy Trading:** Use platforms like ZuluTrade to automatically mirror the short-term moves of experienced traders.
- **Micro-Futures:** Trade smaller contracts (e.g., Micro E-mini) which have significantly lower margin requirements than standard futures, making them accessible for accounts around $100. [5, 6, 7, 8, 9]

## Low to Middle Income (Service & Side Gigs)

- **Trading Affiliate Marketing:** Earn commissions by referring new users to trading platforms. Apps like Webull often pay referral bonuses or high commissions (sometimes $100 per sign-up) for successful referrals.
- **Trading Content Creation:** Stream your live trading sessions on YouTube or Twitch. Even with a small account, you can earn from ads, sponsorships, and community building by sharing your strategy.
- **Prop Firm Challenges:** If you have the skill but not the capital, pay a small fee (often $50-$150) to enter a "challenge" on sites like Goat Funded Trader. If you pass their performance test, they let you trade their capital (e.g., $5,000-$100,000) and you keep 60-90% of the profits.
- **Trade Support Analyst:** Entry-level corporate roles that involve monitoring trade settlements and resolving execution errors. Salaries typically range from $60,000 to $85,000. [10, 11, 12, 13]

## Middle to High Income (Professional Careers)

- **Proprietary (Prop) Trader:** Work for a firm using their capital to trade stocks, options, or futures. These roles are often 100% performance-based or have high profit-sharing splits (up to 90%).
- **Institutional Equity/FX Trader:** Execute large orders for banks or hedge funds. These roles usually require a degree and certifications like the Canadian Securities Course (CSC).
- **Salary Range:** $100,000 - $200,000+ plus significant performance bonuses.
- **Quantitative Developer:** Build the algorithms and high-frequency trading (HFT) systems used by top firms. Requires strong programming skills (Python, C++, Java) and pays $120,000 - $150,000+.
- **Fixed Income/Credit Trader:** Specialize in trading bonds and debt instruments for asset managers like Purpose Investments. [13, 14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19]
